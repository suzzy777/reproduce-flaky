According to [~bmahler]'s trace, I think the problem is related to the recent change in oversubscription and [MESOS-2373|https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/MESOS-2373]. Here is the event sequence:

1) slave starts with no unreserved resources
2) slave uses noop resource estimator
3) allocator->addSlave => roleSorter->add(slaveId, emptyResources);
4) slave receives an estimation about oversubscribed resources, calling allocator->updateSlave => roleSorter->update(slaveId, emptyResources)
5) roleSorter->update(slaveId, emptyResources) invokes resources.erase(slaveId)
6) when we remove the slave turn teardown, the allocator calls roleSorter->remove(slaveId, emptyResources) => CHECK failure.
